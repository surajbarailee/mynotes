Disaster Recovery Plan

Disaster refers to an event or fact that has unfortunate consequences.

Recovery refers to a return to a normal state.

Plan refers to a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something.

Disaster Recovery involves a set of policies, tools and procedures to enable the recovery or continuation of vital technology infrastructure and systems following a natural or human-induced disaster.A disaster recovery plan (DRP) is a documented process or set of procedures to execute an organization's disaster recovery processes and recover and protect a business IT infrastructure in the event of a disaster. It is "a comprehensive statement of consistent actions to be taken before, during and after a disaster".

 

DRP is necessary for:

  • Minimizing risk of delays
  • Guaranteeing the reliability of standby systems
  • Providing a standard for testing the plan
  • Minimizing decision-making during a disaster
  • Reducing potential legal liabilities
  • Lowering unnecessarily stressful work environment

 

Steps for Disaster Recovery Plan:

  1. Determine the scope of your project
  2. Consider your IT vulnerabilities
  3. Conduct a thorough risk analysis
  4. Identify viable recovery strategies
  5. Layout the steps of your plan
  6. Test your plan before disaster strikes
  7. Train relevant team members
  8. Periodically update and revise your plan

 

Strategies for Disaster recovery plan

BackUp only recovery plan

  • Backup files daily
  • Backup technician stores file copied in multiple devices
  • Mostly onsite,removable device and any data center
  • focused solely on data

Cold Site Disaster Recovery

  • Renting secondary space
  • It has basic requirement for running business
  • Installation and reload cause more time consumption

Hot Site Disaster Recovery

  • Identical site set up in remote location
  • Fully equipped with system preloaded
  • Upto date copies of data
  • High cost of maintaining secondary site

Virtual DR

  • Working replica of computing environment is created in VM
  • VM can be hardware independent meaning less configuration
  • Cheaper
