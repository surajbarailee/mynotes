Network Monitoring

Network monitoring is the use of a system that constantly monitors a computer network for slow or failing components and that notifies the network administrator (via email, or other alarms) in case of outages or other trouble. Network monitoring is part of network management.At the most basic level,ping is a type of network monitoring tool. Other commercial software packages may include a network monitoring system that is designed to monitor an entire business or enterprise network.

The network management mainly consists of three parts

  1. The managing entity: It is an application, typically with a human in the loop,running in a centralized network management station. The managing entity is typically a network administer with a system monitoring the corporate network.
  2. A managed device: It is a piece of network equipment (including its software)that resides on a managed network. A managed device might be a host, router, bridge, hub, printer, or modem. These managed devices is monitored by the managing entity for any abnormalities. A managed devices stores all its statistics (like its RAM usage, CPU usage, the bandwidth currently in use etc)  into what is known Management Information Base(MIB).
  3. The Management Protocol: It is the protocol runs between the managing entity and the managed devices, allowing the managing entity to query the status of managed devices and indirectly take actions at these devices via its agents. Agents can use the network management protocol to inform the managing entity of exceptional events (for example, component failures or violation of performance thresholds). A commonly used management protocol is Simple Network Management Protocol (SNMP).
